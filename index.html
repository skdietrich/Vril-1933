Maria Orsic and the women of the Vril Society have occupied a fixed position in the historical record for ninety years — collaborators, mystics, instruments of those in power. That position is incorrect.

Vril 1933 is built on original archival research by Dr. Stephen Dietrich-Kolokouris PhD — research that repositions these women entirely. What the record shows, when examined with the correct methodology, is a deliberate and sustained operation conducted from within the most closely surveilled government in modern European history.

The property fuses this documented research with historical fiction — a narrative form that allows the full weight of the evidence to be felt without the constraints of academic presentation. The result is a work with the credibility of scholarship and the reach of premium drama.
