Dr. Stephen Dietrich-Kolokouris holds a PhD in History from Goethe University Frankfurt — one of Europe's most prestigious research institutions — with a specialization in pre-war Central European history and the occult networks operating within Weimar Germany. His academic training is only half the picture.

As a former intelligence contractor he brings direct field methodology to archival research — an approach that identifies patterns, anomalies, and buried evidence that conventional academic historians are not trained to find. As a CCIE-certified cybersecurity architect he understands how information is hidden, protected, and deliberately obscured.

The combination of these three disciplines — history, intelligence methodology, and systems thinking — produced the findings at the core of Vril 1933.
The NDE — November 2022
Five Minutes.
Three Shocks.
One Answer.
In November 2022 Stephen experienced a cardiac arrest — pulseless ventricular tachycardia induced by sepsis. Five minutes of clinical downtime. Three defibrillations. Attending physicians used the phrase clinical death.

What he experienced during those five minutes he has described in controlled contexts. What he has disclosed: consciousness above his body. A soul-crushing life review. Then a void — absolute darkness — that increased rather than decreased his fear of death.

He returned with one change that has never reversed. The need to understand what consciousness actually is — and what, if anything, survives physical death — became the organizing principle of everything he does.

That need is why Vril 1933 exists. Not as a curiosity. Not as entertainment. As an investigation.
"A documented near-death experience did not reduce his fear of death. It intensified his need to understand what lies beyond it. That need is the engine behind Vril 1933."

They fed false information. They used the trust placed in them as a weapon. They protected something older than politics.

The documentation exists. The evidence is original. No competing property can replicate what this research contains.
